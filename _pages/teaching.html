Teaching and Philosophy

My teaching experience spans undergraduate and postgraduate education in computing and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). I have contributed through lecturing, tutorials, assessment design, course support, and research mentoring, with a focus on helping students develop both conceptual understanding and practical skills in programming, artificial intelligence, data processing, computer systems, and interactive technologies.

My teaching is informed by my research in HCI and AI. I aim to connect theoretical foundations with applied, real-world perspectives, encouraging students to think critically about computational systems, data, interaction, and design decisions. I place strong emphasis on clear explanation, hands-on learning, research-informed teaching, and inclusive support for students from diverse backgrounds.
