My teaching experience spans undergraduate and postgraduate education in computing and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). I have contributed through lecturing, tutorials, assessment design, and research mentoring, with a focus on developing students’ conceptual understanding alongside practical skills in programming, artificial intelligence, and interactive systems. My teaching is informed by my research in HCI and AI, allowing me to integrate theoretical foundations with applied, real-world perspectives.

Teaching Experience

National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST)

As a Sessional Lecturer, I delivered undergraduate courses in Computer Architecture and Organization and Fundamentals of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT). I was responsible for course delivery, development of teaching materials, design of assessments, and evaluation of student performance. This role involved structuring course content to support foundational understanding in computing systems and information technologies.

The University of Melbourne

As a Teaching Assistant, I contributed to teaching across several subjects, including Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Elements of Data Processing, Introduction to Python Programming, and Algorithms and Data Structures. My responsibilities included delivering tutorials, designing assignments and marking rubrics, supporting student learning, and mentoring group projects. I also served as Head Teaching Associate for Machine Learning and Elements of Data Processing, taking on additional coordination responsibilities.

KAIST and Lancaster University

In my research roles at KAIST and Lancaster University, I have supervised and mentored undergraduate and postgraduate students on research projects in Human-Computer Interaction, extended reality (XR), and multimodal interaction. I guide students through the full research process, including problem formulation, experimental design, data collection, analysis, and dissemination of results. These collaborations have contributed to publications and ongoing research in HCI and AI.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach emphasizes clarity, engagement, and the integration of theory with practice. I aim to create learning environments where students can develop strong foundational knowledge while also gaining experience in applying concepts to real-world problems. I focus on helping students build both technical competence and critical thinking skills, particularly in understanding how computational systems interact with human users.

I place particular importance on hands-on learning, encouraging students to actively engage with material through problem-solving, experimentation, and project-based work. I also strive to support students from diverse backgrounds by fostering an inclusive and supportive learning environment where they feel confident exploring ideas and developing their skills.

More broadly, I am interested in teaching at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, Artificial Intelligence, and interactive systems, where students can engage with both the technical and human-centered aspects of computing.
