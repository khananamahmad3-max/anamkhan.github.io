My teaching experience spans undergraduate and postgraduate education in computing, with contributions across lecturing, tutorials, assessment design, and research mentoring. I have taught and supported courses in artificial intelligence, machine learning, programming, data processing, computer systems, and information technologies, while also mentoring students on research projects in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), XR, multimodal interaction, and intelligent systems.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach is grounded in connecting conceptual understanding with practical application. I aim to help students develop not only technical competence, but also the ability to reason critically about systems, data, interaction, and design decisions. In my teaching and mentoring, I emphasize hands-on learning, clear communication, research-informed practice, and supportive guidance throughout the learning process.

I am particularly interested in teaching approaches that encourage students to engage with real-world problems, develop independent thinking, and build confidence in applying computational and design methods to complex human-centered challenges.
