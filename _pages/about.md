I am a Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) researcher working at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and interactive systems. My research focuses on understanding how people interact with digital information, and designing intelligent, human-centered systems that support and enhance these interactions. I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in Human-Centred Computing at the [MAKE Lab](https://make.kaist.ac.kr), School of Industrial Design, KAIST, South Korea. My broader goal is to design intelligent systems that are not only technically robust, but also aligned with human needs and behavior.


I hold a PhD in Human-Computer Interaction from [The University of Melbourne](https://www.unimelb.edu.au), supervised by [James Bailey](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=...) and [Eduardo Velloso](https://eduardovelloso.com), where my research explored gaze- and voice-assisted note-taking to enhance digital text comprehension. Prior to this, I was a Senior Research Associate at [Lancaster University](https://www.lancaster.ac.uk), supervised by [Hans Gellersen](https://www.lancaster.ac.uk/scc/about-us/people/hans-gellersen), contributing to the ERC-funded [GEMINI project](https://gemini-erc.eu), where I investigated eye- and head-based interaction techniques in extended reality.
