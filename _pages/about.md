I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) at KAIST, working within the MAKE Lab. My research lies at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction and Artificial Intelligence, with a focus on designing intelligent, human-centered systems that enhance how people interact with digital information.

My work explores multimodal and implicit interaction, including the use of gaze, speech, and sensor data to support more natural and adaptive user interfaces. I am particularly interested in understanding human behavior through interaction data and developing systems that improve digital reading, note-taking, and learning experiences.

Prior to joining KAIST, I was a Senior Research Associate at Lancaster University, where I contributed to projects investigating eye, head, and body-based interaction in virtual reality environments. I completed my PhD at the University of Melbourne, where my research focused on gaze- and voice-assisted note-taking for improving digital text comprehension.
